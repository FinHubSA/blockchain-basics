Introduction to the XRP Ledger

What is the XRP Ledger (XRPL)?

The XRP Ledger is a decentralized, public blockchain that enables fast, low-cost payments and cross-border settlements. It was designed for financial use cases and uses the native digital asset XRP to facilitate transfers and pay transaction fees.

Key Concept: XRPL uses a consensus protocol (not proof of work). Validators agree on the order and outcome of transactions every 3–5 seconds, making it efficient and energy-conscious compared to mining-based chains.

How It Works

On the XRP Ledger:

  • Consensus: A network of validators agrees on the state of the ledger through the XRP Ledger Consensus Protocol.
  • XRP: The native asset is used to pay transaction fees (destroyed, not paid to miners) and can bridge currencies.
  • Accounts: Wallets hold XRP and can issue or hold tokens (issued currencies) and use features like Escrow and Payment Channels.

Key Properties

  1. Speed: Settlements typically finalize in 3–5 seconds.
  2. Low Cost: Transaction fees are minimal and paid in XRP.
  3. Built-in Features: Escrow, multi-signing, issued currencies, and decentralized exchange (DEX) are native to the ledger.

Common Use Cases

  • Payments: Send value globally with low fees and fast finality.
  • Tokenization: Issue and manage custom tokens on the ledger.
  • DeFi: Use the built-in DEX for trading and liquidity.
  • CBDC & Stablecoins: Central banks and issuers can use XRPL for settlement and tokenized assets.

Ethereum vs XRPL

Unlike Ethereum, the XRP Ledger does not use a general-purpose smart contract VM. Instead, it provides a fixed set of transaction types (payments, escrow, offers, trust lines, etc.) that are optimized for speed and predictability. For custom logic, developers often combine XRPL with sidechains or other systems.

Important: Keep your XRPL secret keys secure. Anyone with access to your secret key can control your account and move your XRP and tokens.
