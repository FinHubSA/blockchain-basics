Trust Lines on the XRP Ledger

Why Trust Lines Matter for Tokens

For most fungible tokens on the XRP Ledger, accounts do not automatically accept balances from any issuer. Instead, an account creates a trust line to an issuer to signal that it is willing to hold that issuer’s tokens [authorized trust lines].

Analogy: A trust line is like opening an account with a specific bank or token issuer. Until you open that account, the issuer cannot deposit their IOUs or tokens to you.

Basic Trust Lines

A trust line is a ledger object that:

  • Connects a holder’s account to a specific issuing account and currency.
  • Specifies a limit (how much of that token the holder is willing to hold).
  • Requires a small XRP reserve while it exists.

When a holder sets up the trust line using a TrustSet transaction, the issuer can then send that token to the holder. Without a trust line, the ledger will not store a positive balance of that token in the account.

Authorized Trust Lines

XRPL also supports authorized trust lines. If an issuer enables the Require Auth flag on their issuing account, then:

  • Only accounts whose trust lines the issuer has explicitly authorized can hold the issuer’s tokens.
  • This effectively creates an allow-list of eligible token holders.

This is useful for regulated assets such as stablecoins, where the issuer must perform KYC/AML checks before allowing someone to hold the token. The issuer authorizes a trust line by sending a TrustSet transaction from the issuing account with the appropriate flags.

Caution: Enabling Require Auth is a one-way operational decision on an issuing account that can only be set when the account has no existing trust lines or offers. Plan your token issuance architecture carefully before turning it on.

Putting It Together

In a typical token flow:

  1. The issuer creates an issuing account and configures its settings.
  2. Holders create trust lines to the issuer’s address for the desired token.
  3. (Optional) The issuer authorizes those trust lines if Require Auth is enabled.
  4. The issuer sends payments in the token to holders over those trust lines.

Understanding trust lines is critical before diving into specific token types, such as fungible tokens and NFTs, on XRPL.
