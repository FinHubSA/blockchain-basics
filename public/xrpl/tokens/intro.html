Tokens on the XRP Ledger

What Are Tokens on XRPL?

On the XRP Ledger, any asset other than XRP itself can be represented as a token. Tokens can model fiat currencies, stablecoins, reward points, in-game assets, NFTs, and more, all settling on the same shared ledger [tokens concept].

Key idea: XRP is the native asset of the network, while tokens are additional assets issued and tracked on the XRP Ledger.

Fungible Tokens

Fungible tokens are tokens where every unit is interchangeable with any other unit of the same token. One unit of a USD-backed token is equivalent to any other unit of that same USD token.

On XRPL, fungible tokens are used to represent assets such as:

  • Fiat-backed stablecoins (e.g. USD, EUR)
  • Reward or loyalty points
  • Other digital-native currencies and payment tokens

Fungible Token Standards

The XRP Ledger currently has two main standards for fungible tokens [tokens concept]:

  • Trust line tokens: The original fungible token model on XRPL, sometimes called “issued currencies” or IOUs. These rely on trust lines between holders and the issuer and are widely used in production today.
  • Multi-Purpose Tokens (MPTs): A newer, more efficient fungible token standard designed based on lessons from trust line tokens. MPTs are under active development and may not yet have full feature parity with trust line tokens.
Remember: XRP itself is not considered a token in this context. It is the native asset of the ledger with its own properties (no trust lines, fixed precision to 6 decimal places, and used for fees).

Issuing Fungible Tokens (High Level)

At a high level, issuing a fungible token with trust lines involves:

  1. Creating and funding an issuing account.
  2. Deciding whether to enable features like Require Auth or freezing.
  3. Having holders create trust lines to your issuing address.
  4. Sending payments in the issued token to those accounts.

The XRP Ledger also includes a built-in decentralized exchange (DEX), enabling fungible tokens to be traded directly on-ledger.

Non‑Fungible Tokens (NFTs)

Non‑fungible tokens (NFTs) represent unique, indivisible items on the XRP Ledger. Each token carries its own identity or metadata and cannot be exchanged 1‑for‑1 with another NFT like a fungible token can [tokens concept].

NFTs on XRPL can represent:

  • Digital art and collectibles
  • In‑game items or achievements
  • Tickets, certificates, or other uniquely identified assets

How NFTs Differ from Fungible Tokens

  • Each NFT is unique and typically has its own identifier and metadata.
  • NFTs are not divided into fractional units like fungible tokens.
  • Ownership is about the individual token rather than a numeric balance.
Big picture: Fungible tokens model balances of the same interchangeable asset, while NFTs model individually distinct items.

NFTs in the XRPL Ecosystem

XRPL’s built‑in features—fast settlement, low fees, and on‑ledger order books—make it a strong foundation for NFT use cases. NFTs can be issued, traded, and held alongside XRP and other tokens, all using the same underlying ledger technology.

In practice, developers combine XRPL’s NFT primitives with off‑chain storage and applications (such as wallets and marketplaces) to build complete user experiences.
